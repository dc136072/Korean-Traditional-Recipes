Kimchi

Kimchi

Ingredients

  • Korean Napa cabbage
  • Korean coarse salt
  • Korean chili flakes
  • Fish sauce
  • Salted shrimp
  • Sugar
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Apple
  • Korean anchovy sauce
  • Cold cooked white rice (Other starch sources)

Materials

  • Extra large mixing bowl
  • Large colander
  • Kitchen rubber gloves
  • Glass Storage jar(s)

Description

This tradional side dish has been made for centuries, both in homes and restaurants, carrying family recipes from generation to generation. By far, this recipe is the best kimchi recipe I have ever tried! It is quick, flavorful, and budget-friendly. The complex flavor and taste is very beneficially healthy for you. It is low in calories and fat while containing healthy bacteria, probiotics, and antioxidants

Steps

  1. Cut though the white stem part of Napa Cabbage 3 inches and dice the cabbage into large chunks.
  2. Rinse the cabbage throughly and sprinkle about 2-3 tablespoons of coarse sea salt over the cabbage. Repeat the process, creating layers of cabbage and salt.
  3. Let the cabbage soak in cold water mixed with sea salt for 2 hours; mixing the cabbages couples of time during the soaking process.
  4. Rinse the cabbage 3 times and drain.
  5. Combine Onion, Garlic, Ginger, apple, sugar, Korean anchovy sauce, salted shrimp, and cooked rice in a blender to make a puree.
  6. Add water to process everything until smooth
  7. Pour the puree into a large mixing bowl and add Korean chili flakes then mix well.
  8. Add the drained cabbage into the same large bowl with the seasoning paste.
  9. Put the kimchi in a airtight glass food storage jar and let it sit for 2 days.
  10. Once you see air bubbles forming, transfer your kimchi to the refigerator and store for 4 days then serve.
